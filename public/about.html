About This App

Notes that work with
your brain, not against it

Brain-Friendly Notes was built by one person who understands that studying with ADHD or dyslexia looks different — and that's okay.

Hi! I'm B. 👋

I was diagnosed with ADHD as an adult — after years of struggling with comprehension, task completion, and a whole lot of other things I didn't have words for yet. I could never understand how people could read so quickly and easily when I was re-reading the same paragraph five times and still couldn't get the full context. My eyes would just scan. Words were there. Understanding wasn't.

My journey with school... well, it's been a long one, to say the least. But I love learning. That part never went away.

I'm currently taking online classes, and I have been struggling. A couple of months ago I called my mom, excited to show her my notes. Two hours of studying had produced a beautiful page header wrapped in hand-drawn ink vines, vividly colored leaves, and absolutely no idea what I'd been reading about. I tried to explain my process to her: "I have to write out each word to follow along, but that breaks everything up to the point where I lose the context." She offered the advice we've all heard. "Just write down the important parts."  ·  "Read it, then summarize it." Love her for trying. But clearly, that wasn't quite working here.

Fast forward a bit. My fiancé and I are trying to study together. We've been at it for a while. I hit a page I have zero interest in — satisfied with my last note page's doodle situation — and I just... can't do it. I was staring at another blinding white page of text that seemed to buzz and irritate my eyes, and I hit a wall.

So I took a break for the weekend. And I fixed my problem. This site is the result. ✨

I've tried other apps. Paid for some year-long subscriptions that are still active. From my experience, they don't quite get it. Yes, the text gets broken down — but where's the color? What if I have a question mid-read? What if something finally clicks and I need to pin that connection before it disappears? Small things. But they make a huge difference when you're actually trying to learn something.

So I decided to make this public and free. No subscription. No signup required. No download. Something easily accessible that keeps the process moving, keeps my hands from cramping, and makes learning actually enjoyable.

I've got ideas to make this better over time and I'll implement them as I can. This is a hobby project and it does cost money to run — so if it helps you, stop by the support page, check out the products, maybe donate if you're able, and let's see what we can all make of this.

Happy learning. And thank you for stopping by. 🧡

— B

How It Works

You upload a file, paste text, or drop in a URL. The app sends your content to Claude (Anthropic's AI) with a carefully designed prompt that turns any study material into structured, color-coded, ADHD-friendly notes.

Your content is processed securely and is never stored unless you choose to save your notes. No data is sold. No ads in the main app. Ever.
